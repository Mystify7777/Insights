Dr. Norman Borlaug

The Man Who Saved a Billion Lives

A Visionary Agronomist

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production termed the Green Revolution. His work saved over a billion people from starvation, earning him the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970.

Key Contributions:

  • Developed high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties.
  • Pioneered the use of modern agricultural techniques in developing countries.
  • Advocated for global food security and sustainable agriculture.
  • Inspired countless scientists and policymakers to address hunger.

"You can't build a peaceful world on empty stomachs and human misery."

- Dr. Norman Borlaug
